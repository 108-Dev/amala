# Facilitator guide - HG Amala Bhakti Devi Dasi
*Amsterdam congregation meeting, Monday 18 May 2026, 20:00*
*Total time: 90 minutes*

---

## Opening (10 min)

Welcome everyone and say clearly why this meeting exists. This is not a planning session yet. It is a meeting to get aligned and make a decision together as a community.

---

## Point 1 - Srila Prabhupada's heritage in Amsterdam (5 min)

You know this history better than anyone in the room. Present it, do not open it for discussion. This is grounding, not debate.

Keep it short and let the facts speak. Once people feel the weight of 1972, the rest of the evening has context.

---

## Point 2 - Why does it matter? (10 min)

Open with one direct question: "What does it mean to you personally that Jagannath is not here?" Let two or three people speak, then draw it together.

If the group gets abstract, bring it back: a community without its Deities is missing its centre.

---

## Point 3 - Build a collective desire to return Jagannath (15 min)

This is the emotional heart of the evening. Give it room. Do not rush toward solutions.

Ask: "Who feels this as a personal calling?" Let silence do some of the work. If Mahavishnu Swami speaks, let it land before moving on.

---

## Point 4 - Find solutions to arguments against the plan (15 min)

This is the point most likely to derail the meeting. Go in knowing the objections before they surface: space, money, authority, timing.

Name the objections yourself if no one else does. It shows you are not afraid of them. For each one, ask the group: "What would need to be true for this to stop being a problem?" Keep it forward-facing.

---

## Point 5 - Write a letter to our local GBC (5 min)

This is a decision, not a discussion. Ask for a clear yes: should we write this letter?

If yes, one person takes it on and the group agrees a deadline before leaving the room. Do not leave it open.

---

## Point 6 - Make a GBC proposal (10 min)

Same discipline as point 5. The group agrees on what the proposal covers tonight. The writing happens after.

Name a lead, agree on what it must include, and set a date for the draft to come back to the group.

---

## Point 7 - What is needed? Who can help? (10 min)

This can run long if people start adding caveats. Keep it practical: space, money, contacts, time.

Ask directly: "Who is taking something on tonight?" Names and commitments said aloud in the room stick. Anything left vague will not happen.

---

## Point 8 - Make ISKCON Amsterdam's early history visible (5 min)

There are things people can do right now: collect photos, find documents, add a section to the website, start a WhatsApp thread for material.

Ask: "Who has something at home already?" End this point with a concrete action, not an open conversation.

---

## Closing (5 min)

Say out loud what was decided, who is doing what, and when the group meets again.

Close with prayer. If Mahavishnu Swami is willing to give the final word, let him. The meeting should end with people feeling something actually started tonight.
