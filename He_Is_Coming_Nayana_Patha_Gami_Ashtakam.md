# He Is Coming
*Ecstatic maha-kirtan. Kheturi festival / Gaura-candrika structure. The announcement song.*

---

**[Suno prompt:]**
```
Ecstatic ISKCON congregational kirtan, full maha-kirtan energy, building from slow call-and-response to explosive full-congregation climax, mridanga driving tempo throughout, hand cymbals locked on every beat, harmonium chord swells, multiple voices — lead and crowd alternating, clapping and shouting, no orchestration, no strings, no production polish, sounds like a live kirtan hall at peak ecstasy, Gaura-chandrika opening section slow and reverent, then tempo doubles, ends at full peak — no fade
```

---

**[GAURA-CANDRIKA OPENING — slow, single voice, reverent]**

Sri Chaitanya brought the name
Through the streets of Navadvipa
Dancing, weeping, calling out —
*Jagannatha! Jagannatha!*

He carried the kirtan to Puri's door
Fell weeping at Jagannath's feet
He is coming through Amsterdam now
His name alive on every street

---

**[TEMPO DOUBLES — full congregation enters]**

He is coming! He is coming!
Jagannath is coming home!
He is coming! He is coming!
Amsterdam, you're not alone!

*[maha-kirtan break — Hare Krishna maha-mantra, full voices, building]*

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

---

**[PEAK]**

*Leader:* Who is coming?
*All:* **JAGANNATH!**
*Leader:* Where is He coming?
*All:* **AMSTERDAM!**
*Leader:* When is He coming?
*All:* **NOW! NOW! NOW!**

*[cymbal crash — full roar — ends at peak, no fade]*

# Nayana-Patha-Gami
*Meditative maha mantra kirtan. Jagannath, Baladeva, Subhadra invocations as bells between cycles.*

**Theological anchor:** *Jagannatha Svami nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me* — "O Lord Jagannath, please be the object of my vision" — from Sri Jagannath Ashtakam (Adi Shankaracharya), recited by Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu himself.

---

**[Suno prompt:]**
```
Meditative ISKCON kirtan, maha mantra cycles as the spine, slow and hypnotic, harmonium single drone layer, mridanga soft and steady, hand cymbals barely audible — present but not dominant, Sanskrit invocations of Jagannath Baladeva Subhadra spoken as bells between mantra cycles, refrain: Jagannatha Svami nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me — sung with deep devotion as the returning anchor, male lead voice only — no crowd sound, no chorus, the mantra continues underneath throughout, tempo never changes, no build, no climax — pure sustained meditative state, ends on a held refrain then silence
```

---

**[OPENING — harmonium drone alone, then voice]**

*Jagannatha Svami...*
*nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me...*

---

**[MAHA MANTRA CYCLE 1]**

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

*[bell invocation:]*
*Jagannatha Svami — nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me*

---

**[MAHA MANTRA CYCLE 2]**

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

*[bell invocation:]*
*Balarama Svami — nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me*

---

**[MAHA MANTRA CYCLE 3]**

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

*[bell invocation:]*
*Subhadra Devi — nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me*

---

**[FINAL CYCLE — slower, voice alone at end]**

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

*Jagannatha Svami...*
*nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me...*

*[held — then silence]*

# Sri Jagannath Ashtakam
*Original text: Adi Shankaracharya. Modernised for Suno — Sanskrit fully intact.*

**Note:** Recited by Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu during his pilgrimages to Jagannath Puri. The refrain *nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me* is the theological thread connecting all Amsterdam Kirtan songs.

---

**[Suno prompt:]**
```
Uptempo devotional fusion kirtan, driving rhythm, hypnotic and meditative, male lead voice chanting Sanskrit with deep reverence — never rushed, mridanga and electronic bass drum layered together, harmonium drone throughout, hand cymbals on every beat, female harmonies echoing the refrain, sitar between verses, the Sanskrit verses are the anchor — the music builds around them not over them, tempo steady and urgent, no key change, final verse slows and expands into full congregation, ends on held refrain fading to silence, inspired by Srila Prabhupada kirtan recordings 1960s 1970s
```

---

**[OPENING — drone, single voice, unaccompanied]**

*Jagannatha Svami...*
*Nayana-patha-gami bhavatu me...*

*(mridanga enters — single beat establishing tempo)*

---

**[VERSE 1]**

*kadācit kālindī-taṭa-vipina-saṅgīta-taralo*
*mudābhīrī-nārī-vadana-kamalāsvāda-madhupaḥ*
*ramā-śambhu-brahmāmara-pati-gaṇeśārcita-pado*

**[REFRAIN]**
*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*
*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*

*(He who plays His flute on the Yamuna's banks, like a bumblebee tasting the lotus faces of Vraja — may that Lord be the object of my vision)*

---

**[VERSE 2]**

*bhuje savye veṇuṁ śirasi śikhi-picchaṁ kaṭi-taṭe*
*dukūlaṁ netrānte saha-cala-kṭākṣaṁ vidadhate*
*sadā śrīmat-vṛndāvana-vasati-līlā-paricayo*

**[REFRAIN]**
*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*
*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*

---

**[VERSES 3–8 continue — full Ashtakam text]**

*(Each verse followed by the refrain. Final verse slows, expands into full congregation voice.)*

---

**[CLOSING — full congregation, then single voice]**

*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*
*Jagannāthaḥ svāmī — nayana-patha-gāmī bhavatu me*

*[held — fades to silence]*
