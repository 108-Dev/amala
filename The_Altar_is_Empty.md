# The Altar is Empty
*Viraha grief — Amsterdam devotees' perspective. Call to action.*

**Mood:** Raw, confessional, ends abruptly — not a fade  
**Theological frame:** Vipralambha bhava — devotional longing as a high state

---

**[Suno prompt:]**
```
Raw devotional rock, sparse and confessional, male vocals strained with grief, opens with single harmonium drone — thin and exposed, slow mridanga enters at second verse, no sitar, no flute, dry room sound, layered vocals only at chorus — urgent and pleading, handclaps that feel like a congregation waking up, bridge spoken almost without melody, final chorus swells into resolve but never loses the wound, tempo slow and heavy, no key change, ends abruptly — not a fade
```

---

**[VERSE 1 — harmonium alone, single voice]**

The incense still burns in the morning
But the eyes looking back aren't there
We dress the space where He used to stand
And pretend we don't notice the air

We know how a temple feels full, Lord
We know how this temple feels now
The altar is empty, Amsterdam —
And we have forgotten how

---

**[CHORUS — voices layer in, urgent]**

Wake up, Amsterdam — He hasn't agreed to leave
Wake up, Amsterdam — He's waiting to be received
The altar is empty but it doesn't have to stay
Write the letters, make the calls, show up today

---

**[VERSE 2 — mridanga enters]**

Prabhupada came here with nothing
No flowers, no fruits, altar wrong
He chanted the mantras regardless
Because Jagannath always belonged

He placed three Deities in this city
With his own hands, without a flinch
And we let them leave without fighting —
Amsterdam, not another inch

---

**[CHORUS]**

Wake up, Amsterdam — He hasn't agreed to leave
Wake up, Amsterdam — He's waiting to be received
The altar is empty but it doesn't have to stay
Write the letters, make the calls, show up today

---

**[BRIDGE — almost spoken, very quiet]**

Every Ratha Yatra He comes back to us
Rides through the canals, Dam Square, the light
Then we send Him back to Belgium
Like it's normal. Like it's right.

It's not right.

---

**[FINAL CHORUS — full, resolves into determination]**

Wake up, Amsterdam — He hasn't agreed to leave
Wake up, Amsterdam — He's waiting to be received
The altar is empty — *it will not stay that way*
Write the letters, make the calls — He is coming home to stay

*[stops — no fade]*
